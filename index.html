The game of Snowbank

     The game of Snowbank is a two-person game where the starting position is a board with squares of color green, red, blue and white, organized in the way shown below. Green squares are always at the corners of the game board, so a square board is always of odd dimension.

    Before the game starts, the size of the grid (height x width) must be selected by clicking one of the options:

     The board is empty at the beginning of the game. And two players, called Left and Right, take turns in placing white tiles on the red, blue, and green squares. The white squares are never played upon. Also, previously covered squares are never covered over.

Neighbors:

     The “neighbors” of a green square are the uncovered red and blue squares that are immediately adjacent (to the green square). When one chooses to tile-over a green square, its uncovered neighbors must also be tiled-over (as explained below).

     Given an empty board as shown in the following figure, a green square in any corner “C” has 2 neighbors (see the upper left-hand corner); a green square on the side of the board “S” has 3 neighbors (see the lower edge); and a green square in the middle of the board “M” has 4 neighbors. Neighboring squares are marked by “N”.

Game Rules:

     After choosing the dimension by clicking one of the options, the game would start. Then, Left and Right take turns placing the tiles as follows:

1. Left can place a single tile on any unoccupied blue square.

Loading

Or she can cover any unoccupied green square while covering all of the green square's unoccupied neighbors (See Rule 3).

Loading

Note that possible moves by Left are indicated by the pointer cursor sign when hover over, while the tile to be covered by that move are shown as whitened.

In contrast, the moves that Left can't make are indicated by the "not-allowed" cursor when hovered over.

Loading

The possible and impossible moves by Right are indicated in the same way.

2. Likewise, Right can place a single tile on any unoccupied red square or he can cover any unoccupied green square.

3. The rule for covering green squares applies to both players. Namely, a tile must be chosen that covers the green square together with all of its unoccupied neighbors.

4. Single green squares whose blue and red neighbors become covered during the game are said to be “snowed-in”. By default, such squares can be covered by either player by using a single tile.

5. The winner of the game is the player who makes the last legal move. (If it is your turn and you cannot make a legal move, then you lose.)
